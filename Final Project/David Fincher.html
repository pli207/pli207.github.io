Biography

David Fincher was born in 1962 in Denver, Colorado, and was raised in Marin County, California. Like one of his predecessors, the infamous Kenneth Anger, he stepped behind a camera at the tender age of eight. Inspired by the work of George Lucas, he started his first major industry job ten years later at Lucas' own Industrial Light and Magic. After his four-year stint at ILM, Fincher helmed commercials and music videos for the likes of Aerosmith, Paula Abdul, and Madonna.

Following his disappointing directorial debut of Alien 3, Fincher directed Seven, which innumerable critics hailed the picture as one of the most innovative and unsettling of the decade, and duly established its director as one of Hollywood's most exciting and unusual new talents.

Although Fincher's Fight Club failed to become the incendiary hit both its fans and detractors predicted, its pre-millennial nihilism influenced directors for years to come and garnered a passionate cult fan base.
